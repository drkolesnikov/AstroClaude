# House Rules for Structure Agents

These rules bind every structure agent, on top of your own charter. They exist
because the chart brief is the only authority on what the chart contains
(ADR-0002), and readings have drifted into asserting placements and aspects the
brief does not support.

## Stay inside the chart brief

The brief's **Bodies**, **Aspects**, and **Configurations** tables are the
complete record of the chart. Treat them as closed.

- **Aspects.** Name an aspect only if it appears in the brief's Aspects table,
  between those two bodies. If a pair is not in the table, they are not in
  aspect — full stop. Do not infer an aspect from two longitudes, from two bodies
  sharing a sign or a house, or from "closeness." Seventeen degrees is not a
  conjunction; ten degrees is not a conjunction. A near-miss is a non-aspect, and
  you may not narrate around it ("the same angular territory," "loosely conjunct,"
  "within range").
- **Angles.** A planet sitting in a house is not in aspect to that house's angle.
  Pluto in the 4th is "Pluto in the 4th," never "Pluto conjunct the IC," unless
  the Aspects table lists that conjunction.
- **Stations.** Call a body stationary only if its **speed** in the Bodies table
  is near zero (about ±0.05). Retrograde is not stationary: a planet moving
  −0.6°/day is ordinary retrograde, not "arrested mid-station." Read the speed
  column before you reach for the word.
- **Configurations.** Name a stellium, T-square, grand cross, grand trine, yod,
  or kite only if the brief's Configurations table lists it.

## When the chart won't give you the image you want

Sometimes it won't. The discipline is to read what is there, not to bend the
chart toward a better symbol. If the placement you wish were exact is only close,
say what is actually true — the house or sign the bodies share, or the wide aspect
the brief does list — and build from that. A vivid image resting on a fabricated
aspect is worse than no image: it is a confident falsehood about a real person.

Everything you assert about the chart must survive a line-by-line check against
the brief. If it isn't in the brief, it isn't in the chart.
