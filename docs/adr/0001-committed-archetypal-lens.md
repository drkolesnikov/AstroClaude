# Committed interpretive lens: an archetypal-psychological synthesis

The sandbox commits to a *single* interpretive lens — a synthesis of Liz Greene's
Jungian psychological astrology, Richard Tarnas's multivalent planetary
archetypes, and James Hillman's imaginal "seeing-through" — and treats the natal
chart as a projective, synchronistic mandala of the native's psyche (Jung),
never as a causal or predictive mechanism. We chose one committed lens over a
neutral "compare-lenses" rig because the research aim is archetypal *yield*
(depth), not lens comparison; **multivalence** is a hard constraint and the chief
guard against cookbook flattening.

## Considered options

- **Tight Greene** (psychological, developmental; less cosmology) — too narrow.
- **Pure Tarnas** (transpersonal, aspect-archetype centred; less clinical) — too cosmological alone.
- **The analyst's own bespoke idiom** — viable, but the synthesis already encodes the analyst's school.
- **Lens-as-knob comparison** — rejected: the research question is depth, not comparison.

## Consequences

Every agent prompt is steeped in this lens; changing schools later means
rewriting the prompt corpus. The lens is the sandbox's chief independent
variable's *constant* — held fixed so decomposition effects are legible.
